Kelly Strayhorn Theater

Overview
My team worked to create a virtual streaming platform the Kelly Strayhorn Theater can use to conduct virtual classes, thereby increasing engagement with both audience and artists as well as generating revenue.

Project Details
Client: Kelly Strayhorn Theater
Context: User-Centered Research and Evaluation Course, CMU
Team: Janie Xue, Piper Hayden, Brent Hong
Role: Design Lead
Timeline: September-December, 2020

Problem
Kelly Strayhorn Theater, located in the East Liberty neighborhood of Pittsburgh, uses creative programming to bring together diverse audiences. With the recent COVID-19 pandemic, however, they have been limited in the their ability to bring live events to audiences. KST partnered with our User-Centered Research and Evaluation class to find ways to increase audience engagement, donations, and attendance at events while maintaining the safety and health of its patrons.

Solution
My team worked to create a community-based website for virtual events. Participants can directly contact hosts and other attendees, thereby fostering more fulfilling experiences and engagement.

My Role
In addition to working to recruit and interview participants, I worked with my team to generate insights that would create value both for Kelly Strayhorn Theater and its patrons. I served as the Design Lead on our project, and was responsible for generating ideas for solutions that addressed user needs as well as guiding the prototyping process.

Process

Client Data Analysis
Our team evaluated attendance compared to marketing method used and the types of events that KST audience members want to see in the future. We hypothesized that by investing into social media campaigns, there is potential to increase engagement with smaller events. Additionally, despite the fact that KST primarily focuses on dance performances, audiences still wanted to see more dance programming. Our team wondered if there was a gap in KST's marketing such that audiences don't know about other dance performances, events, or classes.

Background Research
We decided to complete a diverse range of activities so that we could get a comprehensive idea of the scope of theater and performing arts events. KST is a nonprofit theater that has been experiencing low engagement levels. The low engagement levels have been exacerbated by the pandemic. KST may be able to innovate with digital experiences to increase engagement. For example, using Twitch to livestream performances.

Synthesize, Ideate, & Design
After collecting our team's stakeholder and customer journey maps, storyboards, and prototypes, we began to define our project. Almost every role in the theater will be impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic, but there were now opportunities for audiences to take on a more active role in the theater's programming. We decided to pursue this new problem space to create some sort of platform for patrons to connect with artists, particularly in non-performance events like workshops or classes.

Research Goals

  • Understand what makes people engage in a virtual events
  • Understand how intuitive virtual streaming platforms are
  • Exploring what class offerings will attract different audiences

Think-Aloud
To begin our user research, our team had participants think aloud as they found a virtual event to attend on AirBnb's website. We were interested in learning more about how participants choose which events to attend, including the format, date, price, platform, and activity that they will be completing. There were overlaps in usability problems with the site, including the fact that many users generally relied on filters to search through and find events that met their criteria, but were selective with which type of event to attend and hesitant about paying for a virtual event. Our key takeaways were:

  • When creating virtual events for KST, we need to keep the event’s value proposition in mind and make sure that it is promoted according to the value proposition.
  • We should also carefully consider the virtual platform used and try to mimic an in-person experience as much as possible.
  • Finally, we should consider how events are displayed on KST’s website and how users can filter through the events.

Contextual Interviews
To learn more about the host's experience with the transition to virtual classes, we interviewed five teachers who utilize various platforms to teach classes, as well as the pain points (and what is going well) for the artists/streamers. How artists define a successful virtual event was also a key insight for us to gauge how streaming platforms may contribute to or inhibit the potential of a virtual class. Our key takeaways are as follows:

  • Technological difficulties and limitations significantly reduce the quality of the event and the host’s ability to effectively host the event.
  • Although hosts are willing to try a variety of strategies to increase participation, audience engagement in virtual events is still a challenge.
  • Pre-event promotion is a large factor in determining audience size and audience engagement during the event.
  • Virtual events create more opportunities for increased accessibility and creativity.
  • Setting clear expectations and goals can help reduce the host’s anxiety when planning and preparing for virtual events, and draw in a larger audience.

Speed Dating
After conducting another Walk the Wall session, our team created sixteen storyboards of potential solutions to user needs that varied in level of risk. We then presented these situations to various users to learn more and uncover their needs.

  • For recurring events, participants have a strong desire to build long-term rapport with the host and other attendees.
  • Participants are uncertain about expectations at virtual events, and they rely on the behavior of other attendees to decide what the social norms to follow.
  • Participants would like to attend events with people they are already comfortable with to chat and ask questions during the event.
