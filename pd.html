Non-Black Allyship

Overview
Using persuasive design methods, our team worked to create an intervention to help drive non-Black allies to action to fight anti-Black racism.

Project Details
Context: Persuasive Design Course, CMU
Team: Kraig Fujii, Ally Hopping, Janice Lyu, Divya Mohan, Jenny Xin
Role: Bias Monitor
Timeline: August-December, 2020

Problem
While most of our participants recognized racial bias as a problem, many were unsure or uncomfortable with their role in addressing racism. Some had taken action in their own communities, but nearly all participants admitted to a lack of diversity in their own social circles, and a desire to improve.

Solution
Our design will aim to leverage the existing college infrastructure and shared interests to form long-term relationships between racially-diverse groups of students and motivate non-Black students to take an active role as allies of the racial justice movement.

My Role
I served as the Bias Monitor in the group, a role specifically designated to evaluate and call out how bias might affect our design process and final intervention. Additionally, we all worked together to conduct research and ideate solutions that could best address the behaviour that our intervention targeted.
